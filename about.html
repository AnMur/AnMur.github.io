Biography

Anastasia Batrakova is a versatile concert bassoonist with bright musical charisma and delicate sense of style. In her playing, musicians and press emphasize artistry, aristocracy, charm of spontaneous improvisation and special lyricism. She is widely recognized as a specialist in different musical genres such as early music, ethnics, classics and jazz. She won numbers of significant international competitions in Moscow, Kiev and London, received a special prize for an excellent interpretation of Ignaz Pleyel's music in Vienna.  Anastasia is a founder of the "CONORO" Woodwind Quintet which became a laureate of 1st prizes at prestigious international competitions as well.
  Anastasia is an assistant principal bassoonist with the National Philharmonic Orchestra of Russia by Vladimir Spivakov(2019-present),  principal bassoonist with the Mariinsky Theater(2017-2019)  and a guest soloist  with the Moscow Chamber Orchestra conducted by legendary Russian oboist Alexei Utkin.  In 2017,  she became a guest soloist with the Norwegian contemporary ensemble Arctic Sinfonietta and the Symphony Orchestra of India (SOI) in Mumbai.  Anastasia loves everything related to the early music and often performs as part of the early music ensemble "ALTA CAPELLA" on Dulcian.
  As a soloist, Anastasia collaborated with famous international musicians, among others are Valery Gergiev, Alexey Utkin, Vladimir Jurowski, Yestin Davis, Lawrence Cummings, André Cazalet, Arkady Shilkloper and Andrey Rubtsov.  She participated in many projects with the  Mariinsky Theater, the most significant were "Russian-German music academy in Saint-Petersburg and Berlin" and "Moscow Easter Festival" where she performed bassoon concerto by J. B. Hummel  with maestro Gergiev.
   Recently, different bassoon concerts in her interpretation could be heard in famous music halls of Switzerland - Casino Bern, Finland - Musiikkitalo, Estonia - Mustpeade Maja in Tallin,  Russia - Great Hall of the Moscow Conservatory and the Concert Hall of the Mariinsky Theater.
